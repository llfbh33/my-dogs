Meet My Dogs

Hello All! I would like to introduce you to the fluffiest, loving, slobberiest beasts I have ever had the joy to know! These are my babies! As every parent of a pet knows, animals hold a large section of our hearts and today I would like to help you understand just how amazing and wonderful my sweet little ones are!

Meet Pax!

My sweet boy Paxy is almost six years old. I don't know what I would do without him. He is always there to be my shoulder to cry on when I am upset and he is always the first on my tail when something is going on, he has to be where the action is. If we sit on the couch and ignore him to long he will purposly sit with his back towards us but as close as possible, as if to say "I'm hurt your not paying attention to me or inviting me up" and will occationally turn his head in an, I don't care, type way, but with those doleful pleeding eyes. He loves to play fetch and hide-and-seek. If a visitor comes over, you better watch out, all 100lb of pax will be jummping up on you for kisses and love!

Meet Syl!

Syl, short for Sylphrenna, short for Sylly Goose is the cuddliest little fluff ball you've ever seen. For sure she would go with any stranger who is willing to give her a hug. This is Pax's girlfriend, she follows him everywhere and she loves to cuddle with him. We brought her home when she was 10 weeks old, she is now two and a half years old. She is a lazy brick house, if you sit on the floor she will lay right ontop of you, her snout in your face for kisses. Be careful not to leave the door open while you sleep unless you want to wake up to 95lbs of fluff laying on your legs.

Meet Phen!

Phendoranna, or Phenny Penny as I call her is a nervouse and shy girl, but she has a huge personality. She loves guests, she will sneek up to someone new when they aren't looking and bound away when the person looks at her. When she trusts you though you can tell in her eyes, when she comes up on the couch she has to be on my lap and will lean in untill she is rolling her face on mine. She likes to lay on my chest and stare in my eyes, I can almost tell she is saying "I trust you". When Phen is excited she does the cutest 180 turns, jummping in circles, 3-4 at a time. She loves to play karate, throwing her paws out and bounding. When she doesn't want to eat she will play hocky with he food bowl, pushing it to me, I push it to her. back and forth. Phen and Syl have the best relationship being that they are litter mates. They will cuddle and roll ontop of each other, even now that they are no longer puppies.
